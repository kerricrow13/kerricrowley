Overview

Educational Modality Mentor (EMM) was designed to address a common challenge in healthcare education: selecting learning strategies based on evidence rather than habit.

Educators frequently default to eLearning modules even when other modalities may better support behavior change.

The Challenge

Educational planning decisions often rely on preference, tradition, or convenience rather than educational evidence.

This contributes to module overload, inconsistent instructional quality, and inefficient use of development resources.

The Solution

I developed an AI-powered mentor that synthesizes educational research and guides educators through a structured decision-making process.

Design Process

Research

Conducted literature review and synthesized educational modality evidence.

Framework

Built decision pathways using Kirkpatrick, logic models, and educational planning tools.

AI Configuration

Structured prompts and knowledge sources to support evidence-based recommendations.

Evaluation

Measured efficiency gains and educator feedback.

Results

Early implementation demonstrated substantial reductions in development time while supporting more intentional educational planning.

The project shifted conversations away from "What module should we build?" toward "What outcome are we trying to achieve?"

Reflection

This work demonstrated how AI can serve as a cognitive support tool that strengthens instructional decision-making rather than replacing instructional designers.
