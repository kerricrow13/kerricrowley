FEATURED CASE STUDY

Educational Modality Mentor

An AI-powered decision-support tool that helps educators select evidence-based educational strategies before building another module.

ROLE

Learning Strategist, Researcher, Designer

TIMELINE

2025–Present

TOOLS

ChatGPT, Canva, EBP Research

Overview

Educational Modality Mentor (EMM) was developed to help educators identify evidence-based learning strategies before selecting a modality.

Rather than beginning with a request such as "build an eLearning module," EMM encourages users to first define the performance problem, desired outcome, and context before receiving recommendations.

The Challenge

During an evidence-based practice project examining educational modalities for behavior change, we found that eLearning was frequently selected by default regardless of the actual learning need.

This often resulted in unnecessary development effort, increased costs, and missed opportunities to use more effective approaches.

Research Findings

Our literature review examined educational modalities, behavior change theory, and instructional effectiveness.

Findings were synthesized into an Educational Modality Matrix that connects educational goals, outcomes, and evidence-based recommendations.

Building the Solution

I translated the Educational Modality Matrix into an AI-powered decision-support tool capable of guiding educators through educational planning conversations.

The chatbot leverages evidence-based recommendations, Kirkpatrick's Evaluation Model, planning resources, and implementation guidance to support instructional decision making.

Impact

89.4%

Reduction in development time

$3,890

Estimated cost savings

68.2%

Reduction in development costs

Lessons Learned

EMM reinforced the importance of helping educators solve performance problems before selecting learning solutions.

The most effective educational tools support decision making, not simply content production.
