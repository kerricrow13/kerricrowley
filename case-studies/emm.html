Overview

Educational Modality Mentor (EMM) was created to address a persistent challenge in healthcare education: selecting educational interventions based on evidence rather than habit.

Too often, learning requests automatically result in an eLearning module, regardless of whether a module is the most effective solution. EMM guides educators through a structured decision-making process that aligns learning needs, desired outcomes, and educational modalities using research-informed recommendations.

The Challenge

Educators are frequently asked to solve complex performance problems with a single educational modality. This contributes to module overload, inconsistent learning experiences, increased development time, and inefficient use of resources.

Through an evidence-based practice project, our team identified the need for a practical tool that would help educators move beyond defaulting to eLearning and instead select modalities that best support knowledge acquisition, skill development, behavior change, or performance improvement.

The Solution

Educational Modality Mentor combines educational research, instructional design frameworks, and generative AI to provide personalized recommendations for educational planning.

The tool synthesizes evidence from the literature and guides educators through a consultative process focused on outcomes rather than content delivery.

Design Process

Research & Discovery

Conducted a literature review and synthesized evidence supporting educational modality selection and behavior change.

Framework Development

Integrated educational planning tools, Kirkpatrick evaluation principles, and logic model concepts.

AI Configuration

Developed custom prompts and knowledge sources to support evidence-informed recommendations.

Implementation & Evaluation

Evaluated educator feedback, efficiency gains, and opportunities for future integration.

Results

EMM shifted conversations from “What module should we build?” to “What outcome are we trying to achieve?” This simple reframing helped educators consider a wider range of educational strategies while improving efficiency and reducing unnecessary development work.

Initial testing demonstrated substantial reductions in development time while supporting more intentional educational planning decisions.

Key Takeaways

AI is most effective when used as a cognitive support tool rather than a replacement for instructional expertise. Educational Modality Mentor demonstrates how generative AI can strengthen educational decision-making while preserving the critical thinking and consultation skills of instructional designers.
