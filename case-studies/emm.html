FEATURED CASE STUDY

Educational Modality Mentor

An AI-powered decision-support tool that helps educators select evidence-based educational strategies before building another module.

ROLE

Learning Strategist, Researcher, Designer

TIMELINE

2025–Present

TOOLS

ChatGPT, Canva, Educational Modality Matrix, Evidence-Based Practice Research

The Problem

Educational requests often begin with a predetermined solution rather than a clearly defined performance need.

The Solution

An AI-powered decision-support tool grounded in instructional strategy and evidence-based practice.

The Outcome

Reduced development time while supporting more intentional educational planning decisions.

Overview

Educational Modality Mentor (EMM) was developed to help educators identify evidence-based learning strategies before selecting a modality.

Rather than beginning with a request such as "build an eLearning module," EMM encourages users to first define the performance problem, desired outcome, audience, and context before receiving recommendations.

The Challenge

During our evidence-based practice project, Not Another Module! Examining Educational Modalities for Behavior Change, we found that eLearning was frequently selected by default regardless of the actual learning need.

This often resulted in unnecessary development effort, increased costs, and missed opportunities to use more effective educational approaches.

We needed a way to translate research findings into a practical, scalable decision-making tool that educators could use during project planning.

Research Findings

Our literature review examined educational modalities, behavior change theory, instructional effectiveness, and performance outcomes.

Findings were synthesized into the Educational Modality Matrix, a decision-support framework that aligns educational goals, desired outcomes, and evidence-based recommendations.

Building the Solution

I translated the Educational Modality Matrix into an AI-powered decision-support experience capable of guiding educators through educational planning conversations.

The chatbot leverages evidence-based recommendations, Kirkpatrick's Evaluation Model, planning resources, and implementation guidance to support instructional decision making.

Impact

Lessons Learned

Educational planning tools are most valuable when they support decision making rather than content production.

EMM reinforced the importance of helping educators define performance problems before selecting educational solutions.

This project also demonstrated how AI can be used responsibly to extend evidence-based practice findings into practical, scalable workplace tools.
